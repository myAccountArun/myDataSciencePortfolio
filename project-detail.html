Evaluating G-SIB Communications Beyond Financial Reports

In support of the Bank of England’s supervisory goals, this project explored unstructured financial communications—specifically Q&A transcripts and presentations—from two Global Systemically Important Banks (G-SIBs): JP Morgan and UBS. These materials often contain forward-looking sentiment and early risk indicators that are critical for proactive oversight but remain underutilized. The objective was to develop a scalable NLP-based solution that extracts structured insights from executive communications, enabling timely and informed decision-making for financial supervision.

    • Approach: Hybrid NLP Pipeline for Supervisory Insight

    To address key supervisory questions, we designed a modular, end-to-end pipeline that combines domain-specific NLP tasks with agentic reasoning and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). The solution extracts structured insights from unstructured G-SIB communications, enabling both qualitative interpretation and quantitative risk detection.

    Key Components:

    Data Structuring & Extraction:

    Used PyMuPDF and regex for initial parsing, followed by GPT-4o-mini for structured extraction of Q&A pairs and speaker attribution. Prompt engineering ensured verbatim extraction and downstream consistency

    NLP Task Modules:
    • Topic Modelling: Phi-4 selected for its coherent, finance-specific topic generation across 20 predefined business themes.
    • Sentiment & Tone Analysis: FinBERT and GPT-4o-mini assessed sentiment polarity and tonal nuance across analyst interactions.
    • Answer Avoidance Detection: Phi-4 multimodal model identified evasive responses using synthetic examples and prompt-based generalization.
    • Mismatch Identification: Combined RAG and RoBERTa-MNLI to detect inconsistencies between transcripts and official disclosures.
    • Metric Summarisation: RAG pipeline extracted and visualized key financial indicators (e.g., CET1, EPS, LCR) for trend analysis.

    Agentic Architecture:

    Deployed four specialized agents (Admin, Market Research, Analyst EWI, Root) to route queries, synthesize insights, and populate a traffic-light Early Warning Indicator (EWI) dashboard.

    End-User Products:
  • Power BI Dashboard: Interactive visuals track sentiment, topic volatility, avoidance, and financial metrics across time and institutions.
  • Earnings Transcript Chatbot: GPT-powered interface enables contextual Q&A exploration, backed by RAG and agentic routing.

    • Technical Outcomes and Next Steps

    This project validated the use of advanced NLP and agent-based architectures to transform unstructured financial communications into structured, actionable insights. The Early Warning Indicator (EWI) framework introduced a novel lens for assessing earnings performance, enabling detection of risk-prone quarters, sentiment misalignment, and shifts in investor focus. By integrating domain-specific models with generative AI in a scalable, interactive interface, the solution enhances supervisory speed and interpretability. Initial results from JPMorgan and UBS show strong signal potential, with future improvements needed in retrieval accuracy, discrepancy detection, and trend generalization. This hybrid approach establishes a robust foundation for real-time, AI-driven monitoring of systemic financial communication risk across institutions.
